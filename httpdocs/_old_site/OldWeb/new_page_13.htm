CARING YOUR TEETH IS A LIFE LONG COMMITMENT

As stated by Canadian Dental Association, “taking proper care of your teeth and gums is a lifelong commitment.” It includes a good oral hygiene routine that you follow at home, as well as regular visits to your dentist and an overall "team" approach to your oral care. By taking these simple steps, there's every reason why you should keep your teeth "good for life".

 In this regard, for the benefit of every one I intend discussing certain aspects of dental care in a question and answer style.

Why are my teeth sensitive?
Sensitivity may be due to dental decay in the teeth, exposed root surfaces, broken old fillings, chipped tooth, accumulations of plaque and tartar. Proper treatment should solve the problem.

Why are my teeth stained?
Stains may be due to fluorosis or intake of tetracycline during pregnancy which may affect a baby's teeth, hypoplastic enamel, tobacco and coffee (or) tea usage, improper brushing and flossing. Treatment : some stains are removed by scaling and polishing. Fluoride or Tetracycline stains are normally veneered to look aesthetically better. Bleaching helps in some cases depending on the type and severity of the problem.

Why does bad odour (Halitosis) in the mouth occur?
Bad odour may be caused by gingivitis (gum disease) or periodontitis, bleeding gums, breathing through the mouth, extensive tooth decay with periapical abscess. Treatment: Regular/periodic checkup, scaling with the dentist, proper brushing and flossing.

Why do I get mouth ulcers?
Mouth ulcers may be due to sharpness in the teeth, cheek-biting habits, due to vitamin deficiency, viral infections and other unknown reasons.

Why do teeth hurt?
Teeth may hurt due to mobility of the tooth, extensive decay where the nerves are exposed, trauma to the tooth and wisdom teeth eruptions. Visit the dentist for appropriate treatment.

When do I loose my baby teeth?
Baby teeth normally start to fall from age 5 to 13 years. Totally twenty baby teeth will be lost.

Why do I have a space between the upper front teeth?
This is called diastema commonly due to thick tissue between the two front teeth. The treatment recommended is that this thick tissue should be surgically removed.

Is it possible to straighten the teeth and jaws ?

Yes It is possible and there are different kinds of appliances to straighten the teeth and jaws.

1. Habits breaking appliances: These are used to break the habits like tongue thrusting or thumb sucking. We can do the fixed or removable appliance for this.
2. Space mainteners: This is done if a baby molars are removed when the patient is 6 to 8 yrs of age to maintain the space for the eruption of permanent premolars, again there are different kinds of space mainteners available as fixed and removable types.
3. Removable Dental arch expansion appliance:
This is performed when there is posterior crossbite, to expand the arch whether single tooth (or) more teeth and to correct the occlusion
4. Twin Block appliance: This is done when the patients lower jaw is small and under
developed , to move the lower jaw in front, again this is the removable appliance.
5. Head Gears: This is given to move the upper jaw inside with constant pressure .This appliance is worn 14 Hrs a day

 

Could you tell something about fixed braces?

Yes. This treatment is known as orthodontic treatment.    There are 3 Phases in orthodontic treatment. Phase I - Mixed Dentition Phase: In this the patients are 10 -13 years of age. They do have some of the baby teeth. This treatment takes more time and duration. It can be treated without removing the permanent teeth if the jaw is in proper proportion. Phase II - Early Permanent Dentition Phase: In this the patients are over 14 and upto 25 years of age. They have all permanent teeth. Phase III - Adult orthodontics is over 25 years of age. Sometimes the braces are combined with some removable appliances. After braces normally in 18 - 24 months the patients will be wearing removable retainers for another year as a retentive phase.

 How about Gum diseases?                                                       

We shall see below some specific gum diseases.

About Gingivitis - Causes: Inflamation of the gum around the teeth mainly caused by bacteria in the oral cavity along with improper oral hygiene - conditions - Gum's tendency to bleed.

About Periodontitis: When gingivitis is prolong without treatment it leads to periodontitis. [periodontal ligament joins the teeth to the bone] that results in loosening and mobility of the teeth. This condition grades from 1 to 3.

Treatment: Regular recall scaling and proper oral hygiene instructions should be given by the dentist and hygienist.

What are the ways to replace missing teeth?

The missing teeth, which were extracted due to failure of rootcanal (or) mobility (or) fractures, can be replaced with different kinds.

1. Dental Implants: There are different kinds of Implants available depends on the patients jaw bone. This is determined depending on the number of teeth to be replaced. The Implants can also be used in the complete restoration of the upper and lower dentitions.
2. Complete upper and lower dentures: In this case there is no teeth present in the patients mouth it is restored with removable upper and lower full dentures.
3. Partial upper and lower Dentures: In this case there are one or more missing teeth it can be restored with either Acrylic Removable partial dentures or cast metallic removable partial dentures.
4. Fixed Bridges: In this case there are one or more missing teeth. It is replaced with fixed bridges. The tooth in front and back of the missing place is crowned and the bridges are done. This is done with full ceramics or ceramics fused with metal.

The treatment is determined according to individual patients depending on their needs, esthetics, functional and financial desirement.

What are rootcanals and endodontics?

These services are rendered for a tooth decayed that has exposed nerve inside the tooth. The common symptoms are shooting pain after trigger with air (or) hot and cold substances on the tooth.

Rootcanal is removal of the exposed nerves in the tooth. Depends on the tooth the numbers of nerves vary. First stage for this is the removal of the nerves and then shaping of the canal and the final stage is filling the nerve space and tooth.

Root Canal of the dead tooth:
(a) Root Canal is performed in this case due to fracture (or) trauma to the tooth and the patient unawareness and carelessness that leads to the nerve inside the tooth to become dead and the tooth starts to change color.
(b) Patient negligence of the decayed tooth for long time leads to infection around the roots making the tooth dead.
