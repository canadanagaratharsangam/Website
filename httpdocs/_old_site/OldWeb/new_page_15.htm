Art of balancing the Career (or business) and family

  • When your daughter invites you for an event in her school, is it possible for you to attend?
  • When you assure your wife that you will come home early, can you keep up your promise?
  • Are you doing enough physical exercises at least 4 days in a week
  •  With out any pre planning, can you switch off your cell phone and stop attending to other calls when you need time for yourself in any given day?
  • Do you enjoy weekends with your family?

If you have answered “no” at least for any two of the above questions, it is better to slow down. Otherwise, the price could be anything unimaginable like heart attacks, broken families, neglected children etc.

Do you really want to trade off your life for your business success?  If you know the art of balancing your family life with your business life, you achieve a whole life success that includes your business success too.

Caring self and others, systematic approach, time management and art of delegation ensure whole life success.

As regards to delegation, it is a good idea to seek the assistance of a reputed Financial Planner, who understands your hopes and fears better. To some extent, he will help you to achieve balance between your business and family life. A good financial planner analyses your situation and advises you on aspects like:

·        Managing risk (insurance)

·        Cash Management.

·        Investments

·        Taxation

·        Estate planning etc

Your needs and circumstances keep changing. Your financial planner at periodical intervals sits with you to review it. He can be compared to a family doctor, a generalist. Like a family doctor referring his patients to specialists for particular circumstances, a financial planner may put you with a specialist like lawyer, financial accountant etc, whenever required.

It is interesting to see how your needs and goals, aspirations and circumstances keep on changing.

Up to age 30

  • You choose a compatible spouse
  • Your education starts paying dividends.
  • Your income is mostly secured.
  • Yet you want to have some emergency funds.
  • You want to plan for your children’s education.
  • You want to take life and other insurances to protect your family etc

Age 30 to 45

  • You start thinking of your life’s goals

  • You are willing to take business risks.

  • You have a mortgage and other borrowings

  • You think of life insurance, if it is not already taken.

  • You want to build education fund for your children’s education.

  • You start thinking of your own retirement.

Age 45 to 55

  • You start reviewing your life.

  • Children could become independent.

  • Mostly you have repaid your mortgage loans.

  • You want to focus on your health and retirement.

  • You want to enjoy life through travel etc.

  • You feel the need for a will.

Age 55 onwards

  • You want to be fit and active.
  • Your focus is on your health and happier life.
  • You are free from debts.
  • Your children are leaving you.
  • You want to plan/ revise estate-planning, wills etc.
  • Ensuring regular flow of income, you want to build a capital.
  • You enjoy charity and plan for that too.

In this changing life cycle, a Financial Planner determines your current financial status. He makes a preliminary assessment of your net worth and income. Along with you, he decides whether your stated goals and objectives are reasonable and achievable. If your goals are unachievable, he will counsel you to have realistic goals.  The planner specially prepares for you one or all of the following.

·        Budgeting and savings plan

·        Life insurance needs analysis.

·        Taxation plan

·        Retirement analysis and plan

·        Investment portfolio analysis and plan

·        Estate plan    etc.

It is better to remember always that a Financial Planner’s services are only advisory in nature. Therefore, the success of the plan depends upon the selection of the financial planner and your own active involvement in every stage of the process.
