Stroke

Stroke is a devastating illness that strikes individuals with sudden onset. It disables the person to the maximum during the initial period. Recovery from it is gradual and varies from one person to another. A basic knowledge on stroke will help the person as well the family to understand and withstand the sudden calamity.

Stroke occurs when the blood supply to a part or parts of the brain are altered. The alteration could be too much blood supply to the area by means of rupture of the blood vessel (hemorrhagic stroke) or reduction in blood flow due to blockage of the tube

(Ischemic stroke). The blockage may be the result of blood clot from remote areas like heart or neck blood vessels (embolic stroke) or due to narrowing of the blood tube because of deranged cholesterol and clotting mechanism of the blood (thrombosis)

When stroke occurs the person can loose function of the part of the body supplied by the blood vessel.  Hence the manifestation can vary depending on the parts involved. Usual symptoms are numbness, weakness of one half of the body. Speech swallowing and sometimes eyesight will be affected. Coma occurs in large area being affected or too much pressure manifesting due to blood and swelling.

Death occurs in a small percentage of people. Recovery varies from minimum to maximum. Recovery can be speeded effectively by adopting medical treatment during stroke period. This will be addressed by your physicians.

Prevention is very important in stroke. Few simple tasks may prevent a major tragedy.

Age sex and race are primary contributing factors for stroke. We cannot alter this natural selection. High blood pressure is the most important contributor to stroke. Monitoring it daily by electronic measurement or regular physician visit is important. Weight reduction and reducing salt intake and increasing potassium intake in diet will help to control the blood pressure besides the medication. Normal blood pressure depends on age and other coexisting illness like diabetes. A physician will be able to explain and guide you.

Control of diabetes and cholesterol are very important. Diet exercise and medications again will help along with a personal motivation. Smoking is a very important risk for stroke and heart attacks. Even after stopping smoking the risk can continue for 2 to 10 years. Second hand smoke from others in family or environment is also not good.

People who smoke should think about family and look at others who had stroke. Do you want to suffer like that?

NO. Then all you need is good balanced diet with low salt,  low fat and high potassium.

Regular exercise of 20 minutes a day for three days in a week and maintaining a weight for age and height.  Good control of diabetes, cholesterol and blood pressure to the recommended levels.
